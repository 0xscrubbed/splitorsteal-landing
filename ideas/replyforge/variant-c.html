Uncomfortable Truths About Reddit Marketing

01

Reddit mods don't ban products — they ban spam

The removal isn't about what you're selling. It's about how you showed up. A well-written reply that genuinely helps someone, written by an account with history in that community, almost never gets removed — even when it mentions a product.

02

The founders winning on Reddit aren't posting less — they're posting smarter

They're posting 5-10 times more frequently than the average founder. But every reply starts with the question, not the product. The volume comes from monitoring the right threads — something you can systematise.

03

Reddit traffic converts better than most paid acquisition channels

Reddit visitors who come via a genuine recommendation in a thread they were already reading arrive with intent and trust pre-built. The conversion rate on Reddit-referred traffic consistently outperforms cold ad traffic — if the referral is authentic.
