Three Things Your Landlord Hopes You Never Learn

01

Most bond deductions are unlawful — and disputable

"Cleaning fees" and "damage" charges are routinely applied beyond what the law permits. When tenants dispute them at tribunal, they win the majority of the time. The problem is most renters never dispute.

02

Your landlord entering without notice is a breach of the law

In every Australian state, landlords must provide written notice (24–48 hours minimum depending on state) before entering your rental. Surprise entry without notice is a breach you can act on immediately — most renters have no idea.

03

Landlords have legal repair obligations with hard deadlines

Urgent repairs (no hot water, broken heating, structural danger) must be fixed within 24 hours in most states. Non-urgent repairs within 14 days. Ignoring these obligations gives you the legal right to break your lease or seek compensation — and most landlords count on you not knowing that.
