That person who always underpays.

You know exactly who it is.

The one who orders the lobster and the extra round, then suggests splitting evenly. The one who "forgot their wallet" — again. The one who Venmo-requests you for $12 and somehow never reciprocates.

You've noticed every single time. You've done the mental math. You've had the silent conversation with yourself in the car ride home. But you've never said anything, because that would be awkward.

The experiment

What if there was a way to find out — without saying a word?

Split or Steal is a 30-second game based on actual game theory. At the end of dinner, everyone secretly picks: split the bill fairly, or steal — try to dodge it entirely.

Then all choices flip at once. In front of everyone.

If you split and others split, the bill divides evenly. If someone steals, they pay nothing and the splitters cover their share. If everyone steals, one random person gets stuck with the entire bill.

It's the prisoner's dilemma at your dinner table. And it reveals more about your friends in 30 seconds than you've learned in years.

What happens next

The person who always underpays? They'll either be forced to play fair — because the threat of public exposure is real — or they'll steal, and everyone will finally have proof.

Either way, the awkward conversation you've been avoiding? It handles itself.

No confrontation. No passive-aggression. Just one game that says everything no one was willing to.

What if everyone steals

Not credit card roulette. Better.

Credit card roulette is pure chance. You don't decide anything — a random card decides for you. Split or Steal puts the decision back in your hands.

Your outcome depends on your read of the room. Pick split when others split and everyone pays fairly. Pick steal and get away with it — or get randomly selected to cover the whole bill if everyone defects.

Credit card roulette outsources blame to chance. Split or Steal makes everyone accountable. You don't spin a wheel. You read the room. There's a difference.

How it works

01

02

03

All choices flip at once. The table erupts. Settle up through Apple Pay, Venmo, whatever you use.
